Public Service Organizations – People Management

These organizations are responsible for all aspects of people management including:

  • Integrated business and HR planning
  • Recruiting, hiring, promoting and deploying employees
  • Identifying and developing leaders
  • Providing for employee learning and development
  • Maintaining productive and healthy workplaces
  • Adherence to public service values and ethics
  • Assessing and managing performance (awards and recognition, discipline)
  • Sound relations with employees and bargaining agents
  • Reporting
  • Efficient supporting functions

Graphic description

Visual diagram of Authority Delegation Framework for Human Resources-related Authorities

Parliament creates authorities though legislation. Human resources authorities are divided into those conferred directly to deputy heads and those conferred to the Public Service Commission and delegated to deputy heads.

Authorities conferred directly to deputy heads through the Public Service Employment Act (PSEA):

  • Establish merit criteria;
  • Make deployments;
  • Extend term appointments;
  • Terminate the employment of employees while on probation;
  • Accept resignations;
  • Lay off or retain employees;

And through the FAA, the authority for:

  • Assignments, secondments and Interchange Canada;
  • Hiring consultants;
  • Training and development;
  • Awards;
  • Discipline and penalties; and
  • Termination of employment and demotion.

These authorities may be delegated by the deputy head and can be exercised once the individual in the position meets all the requirements set by Treasury Board and/or the DH, if applicable.
Authorities that are conferred to the PSC that are delegated to deputy heads:

  • Choice of appointment process (e.g. internal or external, advertised or non‑advertised, permanent or temporary, for single or multiple appointments, etc);
  • Area of selection;
  • Assessment, informal discussion, and issuing notifications;
  • Preference for veterans and Canadian citizens;
  • Appointments, including appointments of person with priority entitlement;
  • Hire casual workers;
  • Administration of oaths or solemn affirmations; and
  • Extensions of agreements to become bilingual.

These authorities may be sub‑delegated by the deputy head to individuals occupying certain positions and cannot be further sub‑delegated. These authorities can be exercised once the individual occupying the position meets all the sub‑delegation requirements as identified in the organizational sub‑delegation instrument, including training, and signs an attestation form.

Resourcing

Resourcing begins with you writing or revising a job description. The OC‑accredited HR advisor will review and evaluate that job description and related context to render a classification decision that will result in a classified position at a specific group (or sub‑group, if applicable) and level. Resourcing (staffing) will take that job description and work with you to create a statement of merit criteria (SOMC).

A statement of merit criteria (SOMC) is a critical document in the selection process. It is related to the job description and to expectations regarding the manner in which the job is to be performed. The SOMC contains the qualifications that are essential to the position and the degree to which each must be demonstrated. If required, the SOMC may also include any asset qualifications, operational requirements or organizational needs that would enhance performance in the position. In essence, the job description is the â€œwhatâ€ and the SOMC reflects the â€œhowâ€ the work will be performed.

Pay and Benefits

This refers to collective agreements, rates of pay, and associated benefits. The salary associated with a position is linked to the positionâ€™s group (or sub‑group, if applicable) and level, which are established by a classification decision.


Classification decision

An establishment or confirmation of the occupational group, sub‑group (if applicable), level or ratings assigned to a job, which must be made by an employee exercising delegated/​sub‑delegated classification authority. Classification authority is the power to authorize classification decisions.

Labour Relations

Employees may disagree with their assigned work activities, the content of their job descriptions and the effective date of the classification decision. Their recourse mechanism is the Labour Relations grievance, which must be filed within the prescribed time limit found in the applicable collective agreement (in general, 25 calendar days following the event or decision about which the employee feels aggrieved).

Specifics of labour relations grievances:

  • Work assigned, content of job description and effective date
  • Based on collective agreement
  • Decision can be subject to adjudication

Employees may also disagree with the group and level of their position. Their recourse mechanism is a classification grievance. Classification grievance decisions are final and binding. 

Classification grievances do not deal with the content of the job description or the effective date. Classification grievances are not subject to collective agreements.

Specifics of classification grievances:

  • Position group and level/evaluation.
  • 35 calendar days
  • Decision is final and binding

Where an employee is contesting the classification level, their assigned work activities, the content of their job descriptions and the effective date of the classification decision, the labour relations grievance must be resolved before proceeding with the classification grievance (group, sub-group [if applicable] and level allocated to a job description).

Performance Management

This deals with how employee performance is evaluated. An employeeâ€™s performance objectives are tied to the activities determined by his or her superior in the performance agreement and linked to the job description.

Training

This section deals with the training required to perform the duties of the position as set out in the job description and the type of training that should be recommended in light of the duties of the position.

Official Languages

In the public service, there is an obligation to provide communications and services to the public in both official languages as well as respecting language of work rights, in accordance with the Official Languages Act and related TB policy. The identification of bilingual positions is based on several factors, the organizationâ€™s OL obligations and its OL strategy, as well as an objective assessment of the work to be performed.

Integrated Planning

Human resources (HR) integrated planning is the foundation for assessing and understanding the current and future needs of departments, agencies and the CPA as a whole. Moreover, HR planning is a key tool for making human resources decisions efficiently and effectively.

Organizational Design

Organizational design is an undertaking to organize work to facilitate a mandate or business objectives. It is the process of configuring organizational structures to align an organizationâ€™s processes, systems, technology, culture and people with its ultimate goals. It is part of integrated planning and provides an opportunity to review and revise various components of the work, to ensure the organization is able to meet its mandate and carry out its business objectives successfully.
